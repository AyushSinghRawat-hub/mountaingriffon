Do Dham Yatra Uttarakhand

Do Dham Yatra Uttarakhand

Kedarnath
Nestled amidst the majestic Garhwal Himalayas at an altitude of 11,755 feet, Kedarnath is one of the twelve Jyotirlingas dedicated to Lord Shiva. Known for its ancient architecture and spiritual significance, the temple is surrounded by snow-capped peaks and the Mandakini River, making it a serene and divine pilgrimage destination. The trek to Kedarnath is as enriching as the destination itself, offering a blend of devotion and natural beauty.
Badrinath
Perched at 10,170 feet on the banks of the Alaknanda River, Badrinath is a sacred shrine dedicated to Lord Vishnu. Surrounded by the Nar and Narayan mountain ranges, the temple is part of the revered Char Dham Yatra. With its spiritual importance and stunning backdrop, Badrinath offers a deeply fulfilling experience for pilgrims and nature enthusiasts alike.

Do Dham Yatra (4 Nights / 5 Days)

Pickup Service from Haridwar / Dehradun

Day 1: Haridwar/Dehradun - Guptkashi

  • Distance covered: 220 km (8–9 hours)

Begin your spiritual journey with a scenic drive from Haridwar to Guptkashi. Along the route, witness the mesmerizing confluence of the Alaknanda and Bhagirathi rivers at Devprayag. Upon reaching Guptkashi, settle in for a comfortable overnight stay, rejuvenating yourself for the next leg of the adventure.

Day 2: Guptkashi - Kedarnath

  • Distance covered: 20 km (trek)

Begin your day with a drive from Guptkashi to Gaurikund, the starting point of your 20 km trek to the holy town of Kedarnath. As you ascend, immerse yourself in the breathtaking views of the mountains and the spiritual ambiance of the journey. Upon reaching Kedarnath, offer your prayers at the revered Kedarnath Temple and soak in the tranquility of this sacred location. End the day with an overnight stay in Kedarnath.

Day 3: Kedarnath - Guptkashi

  • Distance covered: 20 km (trek)

Begin the day with a trek back from Kedarnath to Gaurikund, retracing the serene path amidst the majestic mountains. From Gaurikund, drive back to Guptkashi. Reflect on the spiritual experiences of the previous day as you unwind. Enjoy a restful overnight stay in Guptkashi, rejuvenating for the remainder of your journey.

Day 4: Guptkashi - Badrinath

  • Distance covered: 190 km (8–9 hours)

Embark on a scenic drive from Guptkashi to the sacred town of Badrinath. Upon arrival, visit the revered Badrinath Temple to offer prayers and soak in the spiritual atmosphere. Take time to explore nearby attractions, including Mana Village, the last village on the Indian border; Vyas Gufa, the legendary cave where Ved Vyas composed the Mahabharata; and Bhim Pul, a natural rock bridge steeped in mythology. Conclude the day with a peaceful overnight stay in Badrinath.

Day 5: Badrinath - Haridwar/Dehradun

  • Distance covered: 320 km (10–11 hours)

Begin your day with an early morning darshan at the revered Badrinath Temple, embracing the spiritual energy of the shrine. Following this, commence your return journey to Haridwar or Dehradun. En route, take in the stunning views of the river confluences at Rudraprayag and Karnaprayag. Arrive by evening, where your drop-off will be arranged at your preferred location in Haridwar or Dehradun, concluding your memorable spiritual expedition.
